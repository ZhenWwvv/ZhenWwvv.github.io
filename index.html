About Me

Data-driven professional with 7+ years of international experience spanning data science, AI, and evidence-based public policy. Currently pursuing a Master of Science in Computational Analysis and Public Policy (MSCAPP) at The University of Chicago, I specialize in leveraging machine learning, NLP, and statistical modeling to inform global policy decisions and social impact initiatives.

At the International Labour Organization (ILO) in Geneva, I develop scalable data pipelines and interactive dashboards that translate complex youth employment datasets from 100+ countries into actionable insights for global stakeholders. My previous work at Fortune Brands and Nissan (JATCO) focused on supply chain analytics, data governance, and cost-optimization systems, achieving multimillion-dollar savings and operational innovation across teams in Asia and North America.

Fluent in English, Chinese, and Japanese, I bring a multidisciplinary perspective that bridges technology and policy to advance inclusive growth, digital transformation, and sustainable development in the international arena.
